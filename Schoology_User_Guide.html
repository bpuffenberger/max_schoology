Schoology on iPad: A Kid-Friendly Guide

Welcome! This guide shows you how to use the Schoology app on your iPad to check classes, find assignments, complete your school work, turn it in, and stay organized.

Main Focus: how to complete and submit school work the right way ⭐

1. Getting Started

Before you begin, make sure your iPad is charged, connected to Wi-Fi, and has the Schoology app installed.

Tip: If you do not know your login information, ask a teacher, parent, or school helper.

What you may need

  • Your school name
  • Your username or email
  • Your password
  • Sometimes a special school sign-in method

What Schoology helps you do

  • Open your classes
  • See assignments and reminders
  • Read updates and messages
  • Turn in work from your iPad

2. Logging In

Open the Schoology app on your iPad. Your school may ask you to sign in through your school or district.

Steps to log in

  1. Tap the Schoology app.
  2. Choose the sign-in option your school uses.
  3. Type your school name if you need to search for it.
  4. Tap your school when it appears.
  5. Enter your username or email.
  6. Enter your password.
  7. Tap Sign In.
Remember: Some schools use special login methods like SSO, QR codes, or custom sign-in pages.
If you get stuck: Ask your teacher or school tech helper which sign-in method your school uses.

3. Using the Home Screen

After you log in, you may see one of two main views: Recent Activity or the Course Dashboard.

Recent Activity

This shows updates, class posts, and school news.

Course Dashboard

This shows your classes as tiles so you can open them quickly.

Helpful menu items

  • Home
  • Courses
  • Messages
  • Notifications
  • Grades
  • Calendar
  • Account Settings
  • Logout
Tip: The menu button in the top-left corner helps you move around the app quickly.

4. Opening Your Courses

Your courses are your classes, like math, reading, science, music, or social studies.

To open a course

  1. Tap Courses from the menu, or tap a class tile on the dashboard.
  2. Choose the class you want.
  3. Look for materials, folders, updates, reminders, and teacher directions.
Tip: If you are not sure where to begin, check the class Materials area or the Upcoming section.

5. Finding, Completing, and Submitting School Work

This is the most important part of Schoology! This section will help you find your assignment, understand what your teacher wants, complete your work, and submit it the right way.

Big Idea: A smart Schoology routine is: Find it → Read it → Do it → Check it → Submit it → Confirm it.

Step 1: Find Your Assignment

You can usually find assignments in several places:

  • Notifications – tap an alert when something new is assigned
  • Upcoming – shows what is due soon
  • Course page – look in Materials, folders, or weekly sections
  • Home screen – check course reminders and updates
Best habit: Check Upcoming first if you want to know what needs to be done next.

Step 2: Open and Read the Assignment Carefully

  1. Tap the assignment title.
  2. Read all the directions slowly.
  3. Look for important parts like:
    • Due date
    • Instructions
    • Attached files, links, or videos
    • Whether your teacher wants typing, a file, or a photo
Important: A lot of mistakes happen when students skip the directions. Read the whole assignment before you start.

Step 3: Decide HOW You Will Do the Work

Your teacher may want different types of work. Choose the correct method.

✏️ Option A: Type Your Answer

Use this when your teacher wants a written response, paragraph, or short answer.

  1. Open the assignment.
  2. Tap Submissions.
  3. Tap the + button.
  4. Choose Create Submission.
  5. Type your answer carefully.

📄 Option B: Upload a Saved File or Picture

Use this if your work is already saved on the iPad, such as a picture, screenshot, worksheet image, or another file your teacher asked for.

  1. Open the assignment.
  2. Tap Submissions.
  3. Tap the + button.
  4. Select the option to submit from your saved items.
  5. Choose the correct file or image.

📸 Option C: Take a Photo of Your Work

Use this for handwritten work like math, spelling, reading logs, or a worksheet you completed on paper.

  1. Open the assignment.
  2. Tap Submissions.
  3. Tap the + button.
  4. Choose Take Photo or Video.
  5. Take a clear picture of your paper.
Photo tip: Put your paper on a flat surface with good light. Make sure the whole page can be read.

Step 4: Complete the Work

Before you submit anything, make sure the work is actually finished.

Ask yourself these questions:

  • Did I answer every question?
  • Did I follow the teacher’s directions?
  • Is my writing clear and easy to read?
  • Did I finish all parts of the assignment?
  • Did I choose the correct file, photo, or text answer?
Common mistake: Students sometimes tap submit before they are finished. Always check your work first.

Step 5: Save a Draft If You Are Not Done Yet

If you need more time, you may be able to save your work and come back later.

  • Use Save Draft if it appears.
  • Come back later to finish your work.
  • Do not forget to return and really submit it.
Helpful reminder: A draft is not the same as a final submission.

Step 6: Submit Your Assignment

  1. Open the assignment.
  2. Tap Submissions.
  3. Tap the + button if needed.
  4. Choose your submission type.
  5. Attach or type your work.
  6. Tap the checkmark or the button that finishes the submission.
Look for proof: After you submit, check that your work shows as turned in or submitted.

Step 7: Confirm That It Really Went Through

After you submit, take one more look.

  • Does it show your work attached?
  • Does it say Submitted or show that the work was turned in?
  • Can you see the text, photo, or file you added?
Best student move: Never assume it worked. Always confirm.

Step 8: Check Later for Feedback or Grades

After your teacher reviews your work, you may see:

  • A grade
  • A comment
  • Feedback about how to improve

Smart Student Workflow (Do This Every Time)

  1. Open the assignment
  2. Read the directions slowly
  3. Complete the work
  4. Double-check everything
  5. Submit it
  6. Confirm it went through
Pro tip: If you are unsure, ask yourself: “Does my work match what the teacher asked me to do?”

What If Something Goes Wrong?

❌ I submitted the wrong file

  • See if you can submit again.
  • If not, message your teacher or ask for help right away.

❌ My file or photo did not upload

  • Check your Wi-Fi.
  • Try again.
  • Close and reopen the app if needed.

❌ I do not see a submit button

  • Make sure you are inside the assignment.
  • Look for the Submissions area.
  • Ask your teacher if the assignment should be turned in through Schoology.

❌ I missed the due date

  • Submit it anyway if the app allows it.
  • Tell your teacher what happened.

❌ I cannot tell if it was submitted

  • Reopen the assignment and check the submission area.
  • Look for your file, text, or image and the submitted status.
  • Take a screenshot if you want proof.
Remember: Teachers want you to succeed. If something goes wrong, ask for help before the assignment is forgotten.

6. Reading Messages and Notifications

Messages help you talk with teachers or classmates when your school allows it. Notifications help you notice what is new.

To check notifications

  1. Open the menu.
  2. Tap Notifications.
  3. Read new alerts about assignments, updates, or class activity.

To check messages

  1. Open the menu.
  2. Tap Messages.
  3. Open your inbox.
  4. Tap a message to read it.
Tip: A dot or alert may mean you have something new to read.

When should you send a message?

  • If you do not understand directions
  • If you uploaded the wrong file
  • If you had trouble submitting
  • If you need help before the due date

7. Checking the Calendar and Upcoming Work

The calendar and upcoming tools help you remember what is due.

What to look for

  • Assignment due dates
  • Class events
  • Reminders
  • School events

How to use it

  1. Tap Calendar from the menu, or open the Upcoming area.
  2. Look for today’s work and future due dates.
  3. Tap an item to open it.
Best practice: Check Schoology at the start and end of the school day.

8. Good Schoology Habits

  • Check for new notifications every day.
  • Open each class and look for new work.
  • Read directions all the way through.
  • Start early when you can.
  • Save a draft if you need more time.
  • Double-check before submitting.
  • Confirm your work really went through.
  • Ask for help when something is confusing.
  • Log out if your iPad is shared with other people.
Gold-star idea: Make a routine: Messages → Classes → Assignments → Calendar.

9. What to Do if Something Goes Wrong

I cannot log in

  • Check your username and password carefully.
  • Make sure you picked the correct school.
  • Ask if your school uses a special sign-in page or SSO.

I cannot find my assignment

  • Look in Notifications.
  • Look in Upcoming.
  • Open the class and check Materials.

Something looks missing in the app

  • Some Schoology features work better on a computer or in a browser.
  • Ask your teacher if you should use Safari for that step.

I am unsure if my school work is complete

  • Re-read the directions.
  • Check whether all questions were answered.
  • Make sure any file, photo, or written response was added.
Always okay to ask for help: Teachers, parents, and school tech helpers are there to help you learn.
